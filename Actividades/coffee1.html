Itinerary

At the scheduled time, we will meet at Punta de San Pedro, where our journey through the history of Colonia del Sacramento begins. From there, we’ll admire the Bastión de San Pedro, overlooking the Río de la Plata estuary — the perfect panoramic view to start our experience.

We will continue along the famous Calle de los Suspiros, revisiting the Portuguese colonial era, its traditions, and the historical context that shaped this fortified city. Here, you’ll discover the story of Manuel Lobo and the swift Spanish conquest that took place just six months after the city’s foundation. Did you know this charming alley preserves one of the oldest urban layouts in the Río de la Plata region?

Along the way, we’ll notice the contrast between the sober, austere Portuguese architecture and the more symmetrical and ornamental Spanish style. Next, we’ll head to the iconic Portón de Campo, a masterpiece of Portuguese military engineering. We’ll admire its drawbridge and ancient walls while learning how Colonia became a strategic hub for smuggling and trade.

Our walk continues to the Basílica del Santísimo Sacramento, the oldest church in Uruguay. After uncovering its history of fires and reconstruction, we’ll visit the ruins of the Governor’s House, associated with Antonio Pedro de Vasconcellos, known as “The Builder,” a key figure in the city’s colonial urban planning.

Finally, we’ll make our way to Plaza Mayor, the historic heart of the city. There, we’ll reflect on the evolution of Colonia del Sacramento through the centuries before concluding our one-hour guided experience.

After that, Salomé will be waiting for you to taste her special coffee and cakes!
